Analyzing International Football Match Results from 2000 to 2015¶

Author: Aderajew Mengistu¶

Last updated: December 15, 2018

In this project, the entire data science pipline: data curation, exploratory data analysis, hypothesis testing, and machine learning methods will be applied to the international football match results and Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per capita dataset. Specifically, we will attempt to understand how a country's (top 40 ranked men's national teams in 2018) GDP per capita has historically influenced its national football team's ability to win international matches. This will be achieved by uncovering a possible relationship between the national GDP per capita and the team's match performance.

Note: Although football is commonly known as soccer in the United States, we will be referring to the sport as football for the rest of this project :)

Introduction¶

Football, more commonly knows as soccer in the United States, is the most popular sport in the world. It is a team sport played with a ball between two teams of eleven players. The game is played in rectangle field/pitch with a goal post. The objective of the game is to score by moving the ball into the goal of the opposing team. The teams with the most goals wins the match.

This dataset contains over 39,000 mens's international football match results from 1872 to 2018. Many of the matches were organized by FIFA, the international organization responsible for organizing football's major international tournaments. However, the dataset does not include match results from Olympics Games and U-23 or leagues.

Furthermore, this data will be used to obtain the Gross Domestic Product(GDP) per capita for each country in the FIFA top 40 rankings from 2000 to 2015. This data contains the current top 40 ranked teams. We will track the performance of the teams in this ladder dataset from 2000 to 2015 and along with their respective nation's GDP per capita will be used for analysis.

The project objective is to answer several questions pertaining to the dataset and to understand how a countries GDP per capita might influence a match win. For example, which teams have historically won the most games? Is there a relationship between a country's GDP per capita and the number of match wins for given year? Can we predict head to head matchups based on a nation's GDP per capita?

c. Discretize year into three periods¶

Objective: Discretize year (2000-2015) into three time periods and then make a scatterplot showing the mean winning percentage (y-axis) vs mean GDP (x-axis) for each of the time periods. Time periods: 2000 - 2005, 2005 - 2010 and 2010 - 2015

e. Calculate the mean GDP per Capita for each of the periods¶

# Iterate over each team for each year to calculate the mean gdp for each team
result = []
# Iterate over each team in the 'winning_percentage' DataFrame
for index, row in winning_percentage.iterrows():
    sum = 0
    count = 0
    # Iterate over each period in the GDP DataFrame to calculate the sum GDP
    for ind,rw in gdp[(gdp['Year']>=winning_percentage['period'][index][0]) & 
                      (gdp['Year']<winning_percentage['period'][index][1])].iterrows():
        if winning_percentage['team'][index]==rw['Country or Area']:
            count = count + 1
            sum = sum + rw['Value']
    
    # Calculate the mean GDP for each country within each period.
    if count != 0:
        # Store the values
        result.append(sum/count)
    else:
        # If the GDP of a team is not found replace the mean with a zero.
        result.append(0)
    sum = 0
    count = 0

Conclusion¶

Initially, the hypothesis was that countries that have higher Gross Domestic Product per capita would be more likely to contribute more money to their national football organizations. This would result in countries obtaining the best head coaches, training equipment and organzing efficient training camps for their native players. Therefore, a country with a higher GDP per capita will maximize their national football team wins against those that have lower GDP per capita.


In general, there seems to be a weak relationship between winning percentage and mean GDP per capita among the teams in the FIFA top 40 rankings.
